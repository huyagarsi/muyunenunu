Who were charged in Andrea Cincotta’s murder?

In August 1998, Andrea Cincotta, a single mother-of-one and local librarian, was tragically strangled to death.

Her body was discovered by her fiance, James Christopher Johnson, in the closet of their shared apartment in Arlington, Virginia.

Initially, Johnson and Bobby Joe Leonard, a maintenance worker, were the primary suspects in the case, but as leads grew cold, the investigation hit a standstill.

More than two decades later, new developments arose in the case of Andrea Cincotta’s murder.

Who were charged in Andrea Cincotta’s murder?

Both her fiance, James Christopher Johnson, and Bobby Joe Leonard, who was already serving time for a separate crime involving the rape and attempted murder of a teenager, were charged in connection with her 1998 strangulation death.

In a trial, Leonard pleaded guilty to first-degree murder and was sentenced to life in prison. Johnson, on the other hand, was found not guilty.

However, the story did not end there. In a remarkable turn of events, Bobby Joe Leonard, who was already incarcerated, confessed to the murder two decades later and implicated James Christopher Johnson as well.

Consequently, in 2021, both men were charged anew in relation to the case.

This narrative not only encompasses a tale of a heinous crime but also underscores the unwavering determination of a son seeking justice for his mother’s death.
