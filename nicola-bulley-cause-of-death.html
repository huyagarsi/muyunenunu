Nicola Bulley was a 45-year-old dog walker who went missing on January 27, 2021, after taking her two dogs for a walk along the River Wyre in St Michael’s on Wyre, Lancashire.

Her disappearance sparked a massive search operation involving police, divers, drones and volunteers.

But it took more than three weeks for her body to be found in the river, a mile away from where she was last seen.

Her death was initially shrouded in mystery and speculation.

Some people wondered if she had taken her own life, or if she had been attacked by someone.

Her husband Paul’s social media accounts were also hacked after her death, adding to the confusion and distress of her family and friends.

How did Nicola Bulley die?

The most likely scenario, according to the coroner, was that she dropped her dog’s ball into the water and tried to retrieve it, but slipped and fell into the cold river.

The temperature of the water was about 4°C (39°F), which can cause a person to experience cold shock response.

The coroner said that Nicola Bulley did not have any mental health issues or suicidal thoughts, and that she had a happy and loving relationship with her husband.
